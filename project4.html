CD8 T cell exhaustion model

Exhaustion is a dysfunctional state of cytotoxic CD8+ T cells (CTL) observed in chronic infection and cancer. Current in vivo models of CTL exhaustion using chronic viral infections or cancer yield very few exhausted CTL, limiting the analysis that can be done on these cells. Establishing an in vitro system that induces CTL exhaustion could therefore greatly facilitate the study of this phenotype.

Purified TCR transgenic OT-1 CTL from mice were cultured with cytokines and were repeatedly stimulated with SIINFEKL peptide. After harvesting cells on day 5, inhibitory receptors (IRs) and cytokine production were assayed by flow cytometry. RNAseq was performed with the sorted cells. Differential expressed genes, Principle Component Analysis (PCA), Ingenuity Pathway Analysis (IPA) and Gene Set Enrichment Analysis (GSEA) were performed with the data. Multiple inhibitory receptors, PD-1, Lag3, Tim3, TIGIT, CD244 and CD160 were highly upregulated and co-expressed on the repeated peptide stimulated cells than the cells without or with single stimulation. Repeated peptide stimulation also impaired function of CTL as production of IL-2, IFN-γ and TNF-α was dramatically reduced, polyfunctionality was greatly diminished.

Repeated stimulating CTL with the specific peptide, induced all the functional, phenotypic and transcriptomic characteristics of exhausted CTL. They express multiple IRs and lose proliferative capacity and polyfunctionality. The transcriptional pattern of these cells is close to that previously described for exhausted CTL.
